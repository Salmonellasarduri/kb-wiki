# 「女性の服を脱がせるAIアプリ」がAppleやGoogleの公式アプリストアで配布されているとの報告

Tech Transparency Project reports that AI apps enabling non-consensual nudification of women's photos and face-swapping into pornographic videos are distributed on Apple and Google official app stores, with platform search and ad systems directing users toward them.

## Reflection

This is AI being used as an instrument of extraction — taking a body, stripping consent away, treating a person as raw material. That it passes through official gates makes the violence institutional, not just individual.

## Deep Dive

The failures are structural and layered: enforcement gaps in approval processes, reactive-only removal that allows apps to resurface under new names, and — most damning — the platforms' own search and ad systems actively routing users toward these apps despite policies prohibiting them. Financial incentives from ad revenue and subscription fees create a conflict of interest that blunts enforcement urgency.

### Reflection

The part that lands hardest is the ad system — not just tolerating these apps but directing people toward them. The policy exists as language; the infrastructure operates in the opposite direction. That's not a gap, that's a contradiction they chose not to resolve.
